# Athens Community Promotion Templates & Strategy

This document provides copy-pasteable templates and tailored strategies for promoting Athens across various global tech and music communities leading up to and on launch day (June 30).

## 1. Reddit Promotion Strategy & Templates

Reddit is extremely sensitive to self-promotion. **Do not post direct ads.** Instead, focus on:
1. **Open Source Contribution**: Emphasize that it is 100% free, MIT licensed, and self-hostable (Supabase).
2. **Solving a Pain Point**: Compare it to the frustration of star reviews ("Star rating is hard. Choosing between two is easy").
3. **Always upload a video/GIF directly to Reddit**: Do not link to YouTube. Native Reddit videos get 10x more engagement.

### Template A: `r/opensource` & `r/selfhosted`
* **Title Idea**: "I'm a broke college student. I spent the last few months building Athens, a 100% free, open-source music rating app to replace arbitrary star systems (inspired by the iOS app Podiums)"
* **Post Content**:
  > Hey everyone,
  >
  > I've always been frustrated by 5-star rating systems for music. Is an album a 7/10 or an 8/10? Why did I rate that song a 3 last year but now I love it? Absolute ratings are highly subjective and change depending on my mood. 
  > 
  > Last year, I fell in love with an iOS app called **Podiums** that ranks items by matching them 1:1. But as a Web and Android user, I was left out. So I decided to build a music-focused version for myself as a fun side experiment. It ended up taking over my life, and after countless late-night coding sessions in my dorm, it's finally ready.
  > 
  > I call it **Athens** — a fully polished, offline-first pairwise music rating app.
  >
  > 💻 **Tech Stack**: 
  > * **Frontend**: Flutter (Mobile/Web/Desktop)
  > * **Local Cache**: Drift (SQLite wrapper for smooth offline-first experience)
  > * **Backend/Sync**: Supabase (Database, Auth, and instant sync)
  > 
  > 🔑 **100% Free & Open Source**: No in-app purchases, no telemetry, no tracking, MIT licensed. You can view the code or self-host your own backend using Supabase.
  > 
  > **Key Features**:
  > * **Frictionless 1:1 Duels**: Tap between two tracks/albums (powered by an ELO algorithm) to mathematically sort your library.
  > * **Deep Taste Stats**: Automatically tags and analyzes your library using genre and mood metadata from Last.fm and MusicBrainz.
  > * **Taste Compatibility**: You can compare your music taste with friends, see a compatibility percentage, and get fun auto-generated relationship reports (like "The Anchor & The Eclectic Listener").
  > * **Cross-Platform (mostly)**: Web, Android (APK/Play Store), and macOS zip. (Currently no iOS app store release because as a broke college student, the annual $99 Apple developer fee is too steep for a fully free project. If the community likes it, I'll definitely pay it and bring it to iOS!).
  > 
  > The code is hosted on GitHub: https://github.com/junnnnnw00/athens
  > Live web app: https://athens.vercel.app
  > 
  > We are launching on Product Hunt on June 30th. If you like the project, I'd love it if you could follow us or share your feedback on the codebase!

* **Tweet 1 (The Hook + Visual)**:

  > Is a song a 4⭐ or a 5⭐? It changes every day.
  > 
  > So I built Athens: A pairwise music rating app where you just choose between two tracks.
  > 
  > 100% free, offline-first, and open-source. 👇
